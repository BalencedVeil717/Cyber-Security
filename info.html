What Are Cyber Attacks?

A cyber attack is any intentional effort to steal, expose, alter, or destroy data, applications, or other assets through unauthorized access to a network, computer system, or digital device.



Why Cyber Security Awareness Is Important?

Cyber Security Awareness is crucial in today’s digital age. It is the process of educating people to understand, identify, and avoid cyber threats. The ultimate goal is to prevent or mitigate harm to both the organization and its stakeholders and reduce human cyber risk. Cyber security awareness training helps prevent breaches, which can be costly for organizations. In 2022, the average cost of a data breach was just under $4.35 million. In addition, 70% of data breaches involved the human element in 2023. By prioritizing cyber security, organizations can mitigate the risk of data breaches, financial losses, and reputational damage. Cyber security awareness helps educate employees about malicious methods used by cyber criminals, how they can be easy targets, how to spot potential threats, and what they can do to avoid falling victim to these insidious threats. It is essential to promote enterprise-wide knowledge of cyber attacks to halt cyber criminals in their tracks globally.

TYPES OF CYBER ATTACKS

Malware


Malware is a form of application that performs nefarious activities. Some types of malware are designed to create access to networks, some to spy on credentials while others are simply used to cause disruption.
Malware can be used for extortion as well. An example of this can be found in the Ransomware attacks of 2017 where a program was designed to encrypt the victim’s files and then ask them to pay a ransom to get the decryption key.

Phishing


In Phishing, an attacker tricks an unsuspecting target into handing over valuable information, such as passwords, credit card details, etc.
An example of this is a message regarding One-Time Passwords (OTP). A hacker using a phishing method will send a clickable link where a user can submit their OTPs. Once the link is clicked a hacker will have access to the user's personal information.
Phishing is a common form of cyber attack due to its effectiveness and simplistic execution pattern.

Man-in-the-middle attack (MITM)


A man-in-the-middle attack (MITM) consists of a message interception between two parties in an attempt to spy on the targets.
Due to the advent of end-to-end encryption, MITM attacks have taken a dip in the frequency of attacks. Such encryptions prevent third parties from intercepting or tampering data transmitted in the network. Whether the network is secure or not is hardly a factor.

Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS)


In a DDoS attack, an attacker floods a target server with traffic that will disrupt it. Since most servers cannot handle it, it may lead to services slowing down on the website and it eventually crashes.
Unlike standard denial-of-service attacks, DDoS uses multiple compromised devices to bombard the target server, which sophisticated firewalls cannot respond to or are unable to.

SQL Injection

This type of cyber attack targets specific SQL databases. These databases use SQL statements for data queries. In case permissions are not set properly, a hacker can manipulate SQL queries into changing the data if not deleting them altogether.

Zero-day exploit


When cyber-criminals learn of a vulnerability in a frequently used software application they target users and organizations using the software to exploit it until a fix is available. This is called a Zero-day exploit.

DNS Tunnelling


A DNS Tunnelling provides attackers with a stable and consistent line of communication to the given target. The malware used will gather information as long as the DNS tunneling is active. Chances are that firewalls won’t be able to detect such an attack.

Business Email Compromise (BEC)


In a BEC attack, hackers target employees who have specific authority to finalize business transactions. They trick them into transferring money into an account belonging to the hacker.
BEC attacks are the most common, if not one of the most damaging attacks for a business firm.

Cryptojacking


Cryptojacking is used to target a computer to mine cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin. The hackers will be able to get all the cryptocurrency they can instead of the original owners. Cryptojacking is not so widely known but its severity cannot be underestimated.

Tips for protecting yourself from cyberattacks

Individuals and organizations can take simple steps to prevent data breaches and keep their information secure.
The first and most basic step in maintaining cybersecurity is to create a unique and original password for each account. Users should also remember to update passwords every three months.
Keeping up with software updates is important, as cybercriminals often target known flaws in software to access a user’s system.
Cybercriminals may comb through social media posts in search of information commonly used in security questions, such as a pet’s name or mother’s maiden name. To combat this risk, social media users should set their account to private or avoid revealing sensitive information in posts.
A virtual private network (VPN) is a great way to protect sensitive data, especially when accessing a public Wi-Fi network. A VPN encrypts all information transmitted by your device and helps prevent many types of cyberattacks.
And finally, teachers and parents should educate children about proper internet usage. Children and teens should know what the rules and guidelines are for surfing the internet and using social media.
